Why gitban exists

This is how software is really shipped.

When you're shipping a feature to a billion users, software isn't vibes. It's a brutal, unglamorous process most people never see: requirements pinned down, architecture argued out and recorded, designs torn apart before anyone writes a line, code reviewed, tested, staged, and signed off.

That process — the Software Development Lifecycle — is the only reason a stack that size doesn't fall over and take millions of dollars down with it.

Vibe coding throws all of it out: generate something plausible, eyeball it, ship it, hope. Fine for a weekend toy. Malpractice at scale.

gitban puts the lifecycle back. It does the real engineering — requirements, design, build, test, review — and leaves the documented trail behind every decision. You can hand the work to AI and still answer for it: the diff survives review, the docs are there, nothing's a black box you can't defend.

It's the only AI harness you can actually use at work — without shipping something that gets you fired.
